01 Relation between average infection rate and surface air temperature of affected areas.
Scatter plot of infection in regions spread over latitudes This graph is plotted based on the latest data available.
In this COVID-19 analysis, we find out that the novel Corona virus is spreading and infecting far more people in countries which are away from the Equator than countries which are closer to the Equator, i.e., places with warmer climate shows less infection than places with colder climate. Few things to note about the dataset:
  • The dataset contains data from different provinces of multiple countries as well as data from different countries as a whole.
  • Positive and negative latitude values represent the north and south respectively. For example, 40 means 40N and -40 means 40S.
Total Infection to Latitude of Selected Range Graph Plot: This graph is plotted based on the latest data available. The visualization has latitude range of (90N - 60N), (45N - 30N), (16N - 20S), (30S - 45N) and (60S - 90S) for the last 14 days.
Here we have divided all the affected areas (provinces/countries) into multiple temperature regions based on their latitude. Areas having latitude within a certain range are put together as a temperature region. One thing is to mention that regions of the same country might belong to different temperature regions depending on their latitude. These regions or range of latitudes are chosen in such a way that some regions show a steep change in temperature throughout, whereas, the range between 16N to 20S, though has the highest temperature, has almost uniform temperature throughout.

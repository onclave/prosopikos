Dept. of Computer Science & Engineering, University of Kalyani
Department of Computer Science along with Department of Statistics, University of Kalyani, has taken this initiative to develop an interactive site to view and interact with the daily available nCoV-2019 pandemic from various sources. The timeseries data available from various sources are used, especially for students, researchers, data analysts, data engineers and other experts to create interactive graphs, charts and plots to visualize the progress of different regions of India, and / or different countries around the globe and also to understand how various countries and / or regions within India are comparing against one another while fighting against this deadly disease.
Please note that all document / data available here has been compiled from several sources which have been properly linked and referenced to. All information available here is being continuously updated as and when available. If you find any discrepancy or false data / information here, kindly write to us at debabratacse2020@klyuniv.ac.in . If you'd like to add or share some valuable research / information of your own with us to be publised on this site, you can write to us at the same email mentioned above. Your valuable feedback is always welcome!

IMPORTANT NOTICE [ 23 / 3 / 2020 ]: Please note that there has been major changes to the way the original dataset is populated from which this analysis is based on. Since 23 / 3 / 2020, there has been major changes to the way the data is being collected at the source which leads to changes in this analysis. There is no guarantee whether this data will be updated in the future. You may read more about this here. If you have any other sources of data, kindly share it with us. Thank you!

It is important to inform that we maintain our own datasets for the Indian subcontinent. Our team works tirelessly to update the dataset daily by collecting data from various reputable sources, tallying them, and maintaining timeseries datasets as truthful as possible. We have written a few scripts and developed a few tools in order to achieve this. All datasets, tools, scripts and further information about this is available here. You can also view the datasets under the DATASETS tab on this site. Kindy bear in mind that this is in no way, an effort to show the latest updated progress daily data. Even though our dataset and our process of updating the data may provide you with the most recent changes, we urge you to follow covid19india.org site for that, since they are doing an excellent work as a crowdsourced initiative. A major portion of our data update task depend on their dataset as well.

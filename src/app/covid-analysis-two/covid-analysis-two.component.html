Analysis of COVID-19 spread vs GDP Per Capita of affected countries
Datasets The analysis starts with 4 (four) different datasets (mentioned below), containing time series information of day by day confirmed cases, deaths, recoveries, and GDP figures for different nCoV-2019 affected countries throughout the globe. The dataset contains data from different provinces of multiple countries, and in some cases, few countries as a whole, along with their latitude and longitude. GDP dataset contains GDP (billion dollars), per capita income ($) and inflation rate (2019) of all COVID-19 affected countries as of 11/04/2020. Motivation In this analysis of COVID 19, we try to find out whether there is any relation between the virus infection, number of deaths, recovery in terms of per capita income of a country. As we have seen in some western countries like USA, Italy, Spain, France, UK, that the government and people are facing a tough time dealing with this pandemic. On the other hand, countries like China, South Korea, and Germany, are successful in recovering a large number of infected citizens. We are trying to figure out whether per capita income of a country affects the entire population in some way or the other, that determines their way of living, and as a result, how the people of a country are getting affected by novel corona virus. Methodology Initially, for all the countries, their respective per capita income is considered, and along with that the confirmed cases, deaths and recoveries are considered. Since the dataset is time series data, the cumulative numbers standing as per the latest available date (11/04/2020) is taken. In case of the countries, where data is given for different provinces, those data are added up and considered for a country. GDP per Capita vs Infected Cases
Figure 1
In the above analysis (Figure 1) we can see, as the per capita income of a country increases, more is the numbers of confirmed cases. Though there are some exceptions for a few countries. The trend shows that initially for countries with low per capita income, confirmed cases are in range of 100k. But as per capita increases, the numbers rise to 200k. After that there are few countries where the confirmed cases lie in range of few thousands. The maximum confirmed cases are coming from USA. GDP per Capita vs Deceased Cases
Figure 2
In the above analysis (Figure 2) we see, when the per capita income of a country is low, the number of deceased is low. For few European countries, with per capita income in range of 50k, people die in quite a large number. But as per capita increases, the number of deceased cases per country is less, lying in range of few thousands. The maximum cases of deaths are from USA, followed by Italy, Spain, France, and UK. GDP per Capita vs Recovery Cases
Figure 3
In the above analysis (Figure 3) for COVID 19 recovery case we can see, when the per capita income of a country increases from low to high, the number of recovery cases changes from high to low. China, one of the countries, that have reported maximum recovery from Corona Virus, with per capita income of about 10k. We can see the countries, that lie in the per capita range of up to 50k, for them the recovery is high. But as it crosses 50k mark, recovery drastically drops to less than 20k. Conclusion The crucial range of per capita income is the range of 0 to 50k. Most of the confirmed cases, deaths are reported from these countries. These countries have also succeeded in recovering most of their citizens, but in comparison to confirmed cases, they are still far behind. These countries face a quite high death toll. But the risk vanishes for the countries once it crosses 50k mark. There the confirmed case, deaths are reported less, and recovery has been done up to satisfactory level. So overall we can say as the situation goes worse for countries like USA, Italy, Spain, France, UK. Also at the same time countries like South Korea, China they have come a long way, and succeeded in tackling with the situation and recovering their citizens. One of the reasons behind such a pattern maybe due to the fact that citizens in countries with higher per capita income tend to travel more and hence participate in spreading the virus further.
